History

The University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UA Little Rock) is a public research university in Little Rock, Arkansas. Established as Little Rock Junior College by the Little Rock School District in 1927, the institution became a private four‑year university under the name Little Rock University in 1957. It returned to public status in 1969 when it merged with the University of Arkansas System under its present name.

At 250 acres (100 ha), the UA Little Rock campus encompasses more than 56 buildings, including the Center for Nanotechnology Integrative Sciences, the Emerging Analytics Center, the Sequoyah Research Center, and the Ottenheimer Library. Additionally, UA Little Rock houses special learning facilities that include a learning resource center, art galleries, KUAR public radio station, University Television, and a campus‑wide wireless network. It is classified among R2: Doctoral Universities – High research activity.

Campus

The former campus of Little Rock Junior College is now (2019) the campus of Philander Smith University. Students benefit from the proximity of the campus to downtown Little Rock, enabling access to a wide array of recreational, entertainment, educational, internship and employment opportunities that are not available anywhere else in Arkansas.

Academics

UA Little Rock offers more than 100 undergraduate degrees and 60 graduate degrees, including graduate certificates, master's degrees, and doctorates, through both traditional and online courses. Students attend classes in one of the university's three colleges and its law school:

  • College of Business, Health, and Human Services
  • College of Humanities, Arts, Social Sciences, and Education
  • Donaghey College of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics
  • William H. Bowen School of Law

UA Little Rock provides a variety of on‑campus living options for students ranging from traditional resident rooms to multiple‑bedroom apartments.

Student Life

The student life at UA Little Rock is typical of public universities in the United States. It is characterized by student‑run organizations and affiliation groups that support social, academic, athletic, and religious activities and interests. Services offered by the Office of Campus Life include intramural sports and fitness programs, diversity programs, leadership development, peer tutoring, the student government association, student support programs and fraternity and sorority life.

Campus Living Options

Residence Hall
Traditional student housing located on the eastern side of campus.
University Village
Apartment complex on the southern side of campus offering multi‑bedroom units.
Learning Communities
Specialized communities focused on criminal justice, arts and culture, majors and careers, future business innovators, nursing careers, and STEM.
UA Little Rock’s 14 athletic teams are known as the Little Rock Trojans. The school sponsors sports such as baseball, basketball, golf, volleyball, soccer, swimming/diving, cross country, track and field, and wrestling. Little Rock will move to the Ohio Valley Conference for the 2022–23 season.
